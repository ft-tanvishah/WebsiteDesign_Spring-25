About the Book

Charlie, a highly-strung, openly gay over-thinker, and Nick, a cheerful, soft-hearted rugby player, meet at a British all-boys grammar school. Friendship blooms quickly, but could there be something more…?

Originally a webcomic on Tumblr and Tapas, Heartstopper has amassed an enormous online fanbase with over 124 million views to date. Alice Oseman crowd-funded a limited print-run edition, meeting her funding goal in less than two hours. Hachette Children’s Group published Heartstopper Volume One more widely in Spring 2019, followed by Volume Two in July of the same year. Since then, Hachette Children’s Group have published a further two volumes and a colouring book. The Heartstopper series has inspired an outpouring of love since it first published in 2019, with one million print copies sold worldwide to date, sell-out nationwide book tours, and a live-action Netflix adaptation hitting screens on 22nd April 2022, starring Kit Connor (Rocketman, His Dark Materials) and newcomer Joe Locke as the beloved Nick and Charlie, produced by See-Saw Films. Heartstopper follows two teenage boys, Nick and Charlie, as they meet at a British grammar school, quickly become friends and fall in love. Each volume navigates the ups and downs of first love, friendships, coming out, and mental health, beautifully framed by a warm cast of supporting characters.

About the Author

Alice Oseman is an award-winning author, illustrator, and screenwriter. Alice is the creator of LGBTQ+ YA romance comic HEARTSTOPPER, and the writer, creator, and executive producer for the Emmy Award-winning television adaptation for Netflix, produced by See-Saw. Alice has written every episode and been involved at every stage, from casting to music. Alice is the author of several YA contemporary novels about teenage disasters: SOLITAIRE (published when she was 19), RADIO SILENCE, I WAS BORN FOR THIS, and LOVELESS (a New York Times bestseller). Alice’s books have won, been shortlisted or nominated for a number of awards, including the YA Book Prize, the Inky Awards, the Carnegie Medal, and the Goodreads Choice Awards. Alice was named the Attitude Person of the Year 2023, and The British Book Awards Illustrator of the Year.

About the Show

Heartstopper is an adaptation of Alice Oseman’s graphic novel series, following the story of Nick and Charlie, two high school students who develop a deep bond that grows into something more. The series captures the essence of Oseman’s original work, blending themes of friendship, love, and identity with authenticity. Like the comics, the show navigates complex emotional moments with warmth and sensitivity, while exploring the challenges and triumphs of LGBTQ+ youth. Both the graphic novel and TV show highlight the importance of self-acceptance, support, and the quiet power of a genuine connection.

Joe Locke as Charlie Spring

Joe Locke is a British actor best known for his role as Charlie Spring in Netflix's Heartstopper, based on Alice Oseman’s graphic novels. Trained at the Liverpool Institute for Performing Arts (LIPA),his theatre credits include The Glass Menagerie at the Manchester Royal Exchange and The Crucible at The National Theatre. In addition to his breakout role in Heartstopper, Joe has also appeared in Agatha: Coven of Chaos (2024).

Kit Connor as Nick Nelson

KIT CONNOR (Romeo) landed his first film role aged 8 in Get Santa (2014). He played Older heggie in Rocketman (2019) and Joe in Little Joe (2019). His theatre credits include Welcome Home, Captain Fox! at the Donmar Warehouse and Fanny & Alexander, playing Alexander at The Old Vic. Kit is best known for his role of Nick Nelson in the global hit "Heartstopper." Kit voices Brightbill in The Wild Robot alongside Lupita Nyong o and Pedro Pascal. He's filmed a leading role in Ray Mendoza and Alex Garland's new A24 film Warfare alongside Charles Melton and Joseph Quinn.

Yasmine Finney as Elle Argent

Yasmin Finney is a British actress best known for her role as Elle Argent in Heartstopper (2022), where she garnered widespread acclaim for her portrayal of a trans girl navigating love and identity. She is best known for her role as Rose Noble in the Doctor Who 60th Anniversary Specials (2023). She has a strong theatre background, having performed at venues such as the Royal Exchange Theatre and the Aylesbury Waterside Theatre. Yasmin gained a following on TikTok, where she shared her experiences as a Black British trans woman, which helped catapult her into the spotlight. In 2022, she starred as Charlie Acaster in the short film Mars, which premiered at the London Film Festival. She also appeared as a guest judge on RuPaul’s Drag Race UK (2023).

William Gao as Tao Xu

William Gao Hardy (高鑫; Gāo Xīn) is a British actor and musician, best known for his role as Tao Xu in the Netflix adaptation of Heartstopper. In addition to his acting career, William is part of the music duo Wasia Project, formed with his younger sister Olivia Hardy in 2019. The duo, which blends English pop and sibling harmony, has released tracks like Sunrise (2024) and Crescendo (2021). William contributes keyboards, piano, and vocals to the group, while Olivia takes on lead vocals and guitar.

Jenny Walser as tori Spring

Jennifer Catherine Walser is a British actress, director, and natural scientist, best known for her role as Tori Spring in the Netflix adaptation of Heartstopper. She has also appeared in notable television series such as Call the Midwife (2012) and will star in The Next Level (2024). Jenny has gained recognition for her versatile acting skills, showcasing her ability to take on a range of roles in both drama and comedy.

Olivia Colman as Sarah Nelson

Olivia Colman, born Sarah Caroline Sinclair CBE, is a celebrated English actress known for her exceptional performances across a wide range of genres, from crime drama to period comedies and animated features. Her portrayal of Queen Elizabeth II in Netflix's The Crown (2019–2020) brought her even greater international recognition, winning her both a Golden Globe Award and a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress in a Drama Series. Her film and television credits span a wide array of projects, including Hot Fuzz (2007), Tyrannosaur (2011), The Iron Lady (2011), Hyde Park on Hudson (2012), Locke (2013), The Lobster (2015), and Fleabag (2016–2019). Colman has also appeared in high-profile films such as Murder on the Orient Express (2017), The Mitchells vs. the Machines (2021), Landscapers (2021), Empire of Light (2022), Puss in Boots: The Last Wish (2022), Secret Invasion (2023), Wonka (2023), Wicked Little Letters (2023), and Paddington in Peru (2024).

Tobie Donovan as Isaac Henderson

Tobie Donovan is a British actor known for his role as Isaac Henderson in the Netflix adaptation of Heartstopper. Born on June 11, 2002, in Bath, England, Donovan trained at Bath Academy Theatre before making his on-screen debut in Heartstopper (2022). As part of the critically acclaimed series, Donovan's portrayal of Isaac Henderson has been praised for its subtle depth and representation, contributing to the show's global success. He has since continued to establish himself as a promising talent in the entertainment industry. With a growing career in television and film, Donovan is recognized for his commitment to nuanced storytelling and diverse representation.
